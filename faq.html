Frequently Asked Questions

General Questions

What insurance plans do you accept?

We accept most major insurance plans including BlueCross BlueShield, Aetna, Cigna, UnitedHealthcare, Medicare, and Medicaid. Please contact our office to verify your specific insurance coverage.

What are your office hours?

Our regular office hours are Monday through Friday from 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM. We also offer extended hours on Tuesdays and Thursdays until 7:00 PM, and Saturday appointments from 9:00 AM to 12:00 PM.

Do I need to bring anything to my first appointment?

For your first visit, please bring your insurance card, photo ID, a list of current medications, and any relevant medical records or test results. Arriving 15 minutes early to complete paperwork is recommended.

Appointments & Services

How do I schedule an appointment?

You can schedule an appointment by calling our office at (555) 123-4567, using our online scheduling system on our website, or through our patient portal if you are an existing patient.

What is your cancellation policy?

We request that you notify us at least 24 hours in advance if you need to cancel or reschedule your appointment. Repeated no-shows or late cancellations may result in a fee.

Do you offer telehealth services?

Yes, we offer telehealth appointments for certain types of visits. These virtual appointments allow you to consult with our healthcare providers from the comfort of your home. Please call our office to determine if your concern is appropriate for a telehealth visit.

Can I get lab work done at your facility?

Yes, we have an in-house laboratory for common blood tests and screenings. For specialized tests, we may refer you to one of our partner laboratories.

Billing & Insurance

How can I pay my bill?

We accept payments by cash, check, and all major credit cards. You can pay your bill in person, by mail, or through our secure online patient portal. Payment plans are available for those who qualify.

What if I don't have insurance?

We offer self-pay options and a sliding fee scale based on income for patients without insurance. Our billing specialists can discuss payment options with you prior to your appointment.

How do I access my medical records?

You can access your medical records through our secure patient portal. For complete medical records or if you need records sent to another provider, please complete a medical records release form available at our front desk or on our website.
